About the City

Sydney is the capital of New South Wales and the largest city in Australia, with a population of over 5.5 million people. It is located on the country’s southeastern coast and is famous for its natural harbour, stunning beaches, and vibrant lifestyle.

The city surrounds Sydney Harbour and stretches from the Pacific Ocean in the east to the Blue Mountains in the west. It is often called the “Harbour City” because of its breathtaking waterfront views, iconic skyline, and world-class landmarks.

Sydney has a rich cultural and historical background dating back more than 30,000 years, when Aboriginal peoples first inhabited the region. Modern Sydney was established in 1788 as a British colony, making it one of the oldest cities in Australia.

Today, Sydney is a global city known for its multicultural population, thriving economy, and high quality of life. It is home to internationally recognized attractions such as the Sydney Opera House, Harbour Bridge, Bondi Beach, and Darling Harbour.

Beyond tourism, Sydney is also a major hub for finance, technology, education, and international business. Its dynamic atmosphere, combined with a relaxed coastal lifestyle, makes it one of the most desirable cities in the world to visit and live in.
